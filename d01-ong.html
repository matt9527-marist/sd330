My Design Principles


Key Design Principles

"Be as easy as email!"
  • Make all of the content visible.

  • Establish consistency across all of the content.

  • Communicate results of actions with immediate feedback.

  • Use Constraints to restrict users from incompatible options.

  • Provide Affordances to guide users to desired actions.

  • Ensure that the product is accessible

  • Do not make the user think.

Complexity Considerations

  • Do not include too much extra functionality, as it will increase the complexity of your app.

  • Understand the user's potential in using the app.

  • Recognize when there is a need to simplify user interactions.

  • Make the meanings behind icons crystal clear.

Cultural Considerations

  • Take into account the language, common gestures, and emotional aspects of specific cultures.

  • Know what gestures, symbols, or words are offensive or are understood differently by other cultures.

  • Take into account users' comfort with authority or uncertainty.

  • Provide variations of the same product that will be designed to accomodate other cultures.

  • Avoid idioms, gestures, or symbols that are only really understood by one culture.

  • Be aware of whether your audience's culture is masculine or feminine.

  • Be aware that, for different languages, people may read in a different direction entirely.

  • Design inclusively.

Conceptional Considerations

  • Strategize a familiar framework for your app using a conceptional model

  • Make use of metaphors that make interacting with your app familiar, with affordances and constraints.

  • Design the conceptual model purposely.

  • Place familiarity over novelty, being aware of the drawbacks that can result from introducing something new.

  • Design the conceptual model with the users in mind, instead of letting it simply arise from the technology.

  • Empower the users to continue using familiar versions of an app for a limited time when making updates.

  • Ensure the conceptual model actually fits the mental model.

  • Do not make the user think about how a certain button or funciton works.

Human Centered Design

  • Provide discoverability, such that users can determine what actions are possible.

  • Provide feedback!

  • Make use of a conceptual model fitting a familiar mental model.

  • Include affordances, signifiers, and constraints.

  • Make good mappings between the controls and their actions.

  • Design with empathy and psychological understanding of your users.

  • Place the users' needs and capabilities first.

  • Let your app communicate with the users.

Cognitive Design Principles

  • Make information salient when it requires attention at a given stage of a task.

  • Avoid cluttering interfaces with information.

  • Categorize and space out items of information to avoid producing a noisy interface.

  • Use proper color contrast.

  • Utilize haptic feedback judiciously.

  • Reduce cognitive load for users by avoiding long and complicated procedures for completing tasks.

  • Promote recognition over recall.

  • Use progressive disclosure - presenting only increments of information so as to not overload the user.

  • Deliver what the user expects based on other sites or similar apps.

  • Encourage exploration within your interfaces.

  • Constrain and guide users to select appropriate actions when initially learning.

Accessibility Aspects

  • Design products that are perceivable, operable, understandable, and robust.

  • Give alerts and visual cues.

  • Provide clear instructions when asking for input.

  • Include a text-to-speech option or provide a way to enlarge the font.

  • Allow for keyboard-only input for users who cannot use a mouse cursor.

  • Label colors that are important.

  • Build in user-friendliness.

  • Take into account both mental and physical disabilities.

Emotional Aspects

  • Persuade using stories or anecdotes.

  • Influence people to take the right actions by influencing their emotions.

  • Include pleasant surprises.

  • Create ways for users to fix their own mistakes.

  • Ensure the look and feel of the app promotes positive emotions from the start.

Interface Design

  • Make the product self-explanatory, obvious.

  • Take advantage of conventions, and support hierarchies of information.

  • Eliminate distractions.

  • Clarify what is and is not clickable.

  • Break pages up into clearly-defined areas.

  • Keep paragraphs short.

  • Use bulleted lists, tables, and clear headings.

Aesthetics and Mobile Design

  • Have one scalable design.

  • Allow for scanning.

  • Enable the user to navigate one-handed.

  • Do not use redirects into separate windows.
